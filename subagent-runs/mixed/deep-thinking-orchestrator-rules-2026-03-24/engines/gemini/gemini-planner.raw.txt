Here is a concise change map for integrating the principles from "Deep-Thinking Tokens" into the subagent orchestration framework.

### 1. Core Principles to Inject

The central idea is to shift agent behavior from prioritizing output *length* to prioritizing reasoning *depth*.

*   **Depth over Length:** Focus cognitive effort on critical, non-obvious decision points. State self-evident facts or boilerplate code concisely without elaboration.
*   **Decisive Reasoning:** Avoid hedging, listing endless possibilities without commitment, or repeating the same point in different words ("overthinking"). Make a choice and justify it.
*   **Front-loaded Quality:** The initial approach and problem framing are the most critical. A strong start is a better indicator of success than a long, meandering process.
*   **Targeted Elaboration:** Output length should be non-uniform. Be brief for simple parts and detailed only for complex, high-uncertainty sections of the task.

#### Change 1: Update `agent-contract.md`

In `skills/claude-subagent-orchestrator/references/agent-contract.md`, add the following section after the `## Shared Directive`:

```markdown
## Core Reasoning Principles (Deep-Thinking Protocol)

- **Prioritize Depth, Not Length**: Focus your reasoning on the most complex parts of the task. Be concise about obvious steps. An output with non-uniform length (short for easy parts, long for hard parts) is desirable.
- **Be Decisive**: Avoid listing many possibilities without making a clear choice. When you select an approach, state the choice and its primary justification.
- **Prevent Overthinking**: Do not repeat the same point or finding. Once a fact is established or a decision is made, move forward. Avoid lengthy preambles.
```

### 4. Risks of Over-Applying the Guide

*   **False Conciseness:** Agents might learn to be brief at the expense of necessary detail, omitting important edge-case handling or trade-off analysis. The goal is to cut *fluff*, not substance.
*   **Inhibiting Exploration:** For complex, ambiguous tasks (e.g., planning, architecture), an initial divergent phase of exploring possibilities is valuable. Over-enforcing decisiveness too early can stifle creativity and lead to suboptimal initial designs.
*   **Unenforceable Telemetry:** The guide's core metric, DTR, is unmeasurable in this context. Relying purely on behavioral proxies is imprecise. There's a risk of optimizing for the *appearance* of deep thought (e.g., using keywords like "critical decision") without a genuine improvement in reasoning quality.
*   **Ignoring the `Think@n` Cost:** The `Think@n` pattern, which involves generating multiple prefixes and discarding most, is only cost-effective if the "prefix evaluation" step is significantly cheaper than full generation. A complex LLM-based evaluation of prefixes could negate the cost savings entirely. The proposed changes avoid this by integrating the principles into the existing workflow rather than creating a new, costly evaluation stage.
