### `project-status/current.md` — Purpose: Operational source-of-truth / handoff

**Core problem: no fast snapshot at the top.**

The file is 100+ lines of dense reverse-chronological prose with zero headers. The very first thing a follow-up worker sees is a paragraph about Hall curation Phase A from 2026-04-01. There's no visible answer to "what project is this?", "what's the current state?", or "what's next?" without reading through everything.

For a handoff document, the reader needs the ground truth in the first screen, not buried in the log.

**Fix needed:** Add a pinned header block before the log entries:

```markdown
## Current State (2026-04-05)
- **Active project:** mostria.com (vibe-web)  
- **Status:** Hall Curation Phase A closed; Phase B (repository integration + UI) pending  
- **Next tasks:** [list them here]

---

```

The log entries themselves are well-written — the problem is purely structural.
