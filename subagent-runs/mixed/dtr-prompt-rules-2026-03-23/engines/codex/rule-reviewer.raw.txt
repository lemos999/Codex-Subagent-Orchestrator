**Highest-Risk Wording Gaps**
- `Remaining uncertainty` / `uncertainties` in implementer returns: pushes agents to narrate doubt even after they should stop.
- `recommendations` in explorer outputs: invites option lists without a selected answer.
- `structured markdown` in planner outputs: encourages completeness theater unless paired with “pick one approach and justify briefly.”
- `specific findings` without “material only” or “evidence required”: invites exhaustive issue-dumping.
- Broad criteria like `logic sound, edge cases handled`: good as coverage prompts, weak as contracts for decisive evidence-based review.

**Reviewer Criteria Suggestions**
- Require `Verdict first`, then only material findings.
- Require every finding to include `evidence`, such as file/line or concrete symptom, plus one fix direction.
- Fail outputs that present multiple possibilities without naming the chosen one.
- Fail outputs that repeat the same issue in different wording.
- Add a sufficiency check: “If confidence is already sufficient to accept/reject, stop; do not enumerate non-blocking possibilities.”
- For planner/watchdog review, require one line on “why this choice over the strongest alternative.”

**What Should Not Be Changed**
- Do not weaken explicit writable scopes, stop conditions, self-validation, or fix/re-review loops; those are already high-value constraints.
- Do not add long anti-overthinking text to every template separately; centralize it in the contract/rubric layer and keep templates lean.
- Do not force exhaustive alternative analysis everywhere; only planner/watchdog/reviewer paths need explicit choice-vs-alternative checks.
- Do not replace the current economy and scope rules with verbose style rules; the issue is output discipline, not missing process structure.
