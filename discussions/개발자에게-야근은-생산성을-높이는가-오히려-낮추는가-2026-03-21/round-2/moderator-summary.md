## Round Summary

### Participant Positions

**Claude**
- Sleep debt accumulates non-linearly; 10 days of 6-hour sleep equals 24-hour total sleep deprivation
- Architecture/design roles most vulnerable to sleep loss; recovery requires 7-14 days, not just weekends
- "Short-term exceptions" show measurable cognitive decline in knowledge work after 3+ consecutive days

**Codex**
- Overtime has limited effectiveness for procedural, short-term tasks only
- Repeated overtime across teams causes sustained productivity loss

**Gemini**
- Chronic overwork degrades cognitive function, increases errors, reduces output quality
- Widespread overwork erodes psychological safety, trust, communication, and team morale—driving talent retention issues and organizational dysfunction

### Areas of Agreement
- Overtime is **not sustainable** for long-term productivity
- Repeated/chronic patterns cause greater harm than single events
- Individual cognitive decline and organizational health are interconnected

### Areas of Disagreement
- **Scope of "short-term validity"**: Claude argues 3+ days shows measurable decline; Codex allows "limited effectiveness" for procedural work (specificity unclear)
- **Role-based differential impact**: Claude emphasizes role differences; Codex/Gemini treat impact more uniformly across team types

### Open Questions
- How do procedure-based vs. knowledge-work roles operationally differ in real projects under overtime?
- What threshold duration shifts from "exception" to "harmful pattern" in different organizational contexts?
- Are recovery protocols (sleep scheduling, task rotation) measurable interventions that change the equation?
