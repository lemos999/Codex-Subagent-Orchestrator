## Summary of Positions

**Claude:**
Overwork systematically lowers productivity due to sleep debt and prefrontal cortex damage. Short-term overtime (1-2 days max) may be necessary, but chronic patterns accumulate cognitive deficit and technical debt. Recommends limiting to 1-2 times weekly with 24-48 hour recovery periods.

**Codex:**
Overwork can serve exceptional short-term needs but reduces sustained team productivity over time. (Brief position; limited detail provided.)

**Gemini:**
Long-term overwork reduces productivity through fatigue-induced cognitive decline, error increase, and burnout risk. Short-term deadlines may justify it, but repeated patterns damage sustainability and organizational health. Requires addressing root causes: realistic goals, clear priorities, and health-focused culture.

---

## Areas of Agreement
- All three reject overwork as a sustainable productivity strategy
- All acknowledge short-term exceptions exist (deadlines, emergencies)
- All point to degraded cognitive function and quality issues as core problems

## Areas of Disagreement
- **Scope of detail**: Claude provides neuroscience mechanisms; Gemini emphasizes organizational culture; Codex offers minimal elaboration
- **Recovery model**: Claude specifies 24-48 hour windows; Gemini focuses on culture change as prevention; Codex offers no recovery recommendation

## Open Questions
- What constitutes "short-term" — days? weeks?
- How do individual variation, role type (QA vs. architecture), and deadline predictability affect the calculus?
- What measurable metrics (defect rate, velocity, retention) best track productivity cost?
- How do organizations transition away from overwork culture once established?
