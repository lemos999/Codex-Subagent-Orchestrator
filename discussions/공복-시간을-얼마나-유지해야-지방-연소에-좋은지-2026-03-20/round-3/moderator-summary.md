## Summary

### Claude's Position
- **Stratified recommendations by metabolic health**: 12-16h for healthy adults; 16-18h for insulin-resistant/overweight groups
- **Key variable**: Insulin sensitivity determines efficiency of fat-burning at same fasting duration; longer fasting may be clinically necessary for metabolic compensation in insulin-resistant populations
- **Adjusted stance**: "12-16h sufficient" applies mainly to metabolically healthy people

### Codex's Position
- **12-16 hours is sufficient for most people**; 16-18 hours is a situational option, not universal optimum
- Emphasizes **sustainability and practicality over marginal gains**
- Position remains concise and pragmatic-focused

### Gemini's Position
- **12-16 hours daily is optimal practical range** for fat burning with acceptable risk profile
- Beyond 16h shows **diminishing returns with increased risks**; requires diet quality and professional oversight
- New insight: **Eating window nutrient composition** critically impacts fat burning efficiency and muscle preservation

## Agreement
- 12-16 hours is effective for fat burning

- Sustainability is important

## Disagreement
- **Whether 16-18h is "better" vs. "risky"**: Claude sees clinical benefit for specific metabolic groups; Codex/Gemini emphasize risks/diminishing returns
- **Personalization basis**: Claude focuses on metabolic status stratification; Codex/Gemini favor general safety guidelines
