## Position Summaries

**Claude:** Speed is absolute priority; users/market define quality, not teams. Technical debt management is irrelevant until PMF. Regulated sectors can defer compliance by narrowing scope and launching in non-regulated areas first.

**Codex:** Fast MVP is necessary, but non-reversible areas (trust, security, compliance, data integrity) require high standards from day one. These don't slow execution; they prevent catastrophic later costs.

**Gemini:** Rapid validated MVP is paramount. "Rapid" and "validated" must accommodate essential regulatory/safety requirements. Technical debt management becomes critical at PMF or scaling, not before.

---

## Convergence & Divergence

**Agreement:**
- Speed to market is essential in early stage
- User/market validation should drive prioritization
- Technical debt tolerance is high pre-PMF

**Disagreement:**
- **Regulatory scope:** Claude advocates scope reduction to avoid compliance; Codex/Gemini argue essential requirements must be met from day one
- **Quality baselines:** Claude minimizes upfront quality; Codex/Gemini identify critical areas (security, data integrity) that require standards initially
- **Proactive debt management:** Claude defers entirely until PMF; Codex/Gemini suggest some management is necessary during execution

**Open Questions:**
1. Which quality areas are truly "non-reversible" vs. which can be technical debt?
2. For regulated industries, what constitutes an MVP-compliant minimum?
3. Does scope reduction (Claude) genuinely avoid compliance work, or merely delay it?
